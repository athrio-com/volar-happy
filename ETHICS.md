# Code of Ethics

Loom is released under the MIT License, which places no legal restriction on
how the software is used. This document is separate from the license. It is a
statement of the intent behind the project and a request to its users.

## Intent

Loom exists to make programs easier to read, reason about, and learn from. It
is built in the open so that anyone can benefit from it, contribute to it, and
adapt it.

## Uses we ask you not to pursue

We ask that Loom not be used to build, operate, or directly support:

- Adult-entertainment platforms and sex-work intermediaries
- Gambling products, including online casinos, sportsbooks, lootbox systems,
  and "social casino" mechanics
- Weapons systems, munitions, or autonomous targeting of any kind
- Military command, control, navigation, surveillance, or targeting systems
- Mass surveillance of civilian populations
- Systems designed to deceive, manipulate, or coerce users

## Why a request and not a clause

Field-of-use restrictions in source licenses tend to be hard to define, hard
to enforce, and harmful to adoption in ways that ultimately hurt the people
they are meant to help. We prefer to be clear about our values, trust users
to act on their own, and reserve our own time and attention for work we
believe in.

You retain every right granted to you by the MIT License regardless of this
document.

## Contact

If you have questions about whether a particular use is in the spirit of this
document, open an issue or reach out to the maintainers.
